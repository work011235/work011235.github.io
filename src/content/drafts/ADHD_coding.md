# Failing Fast with Vibe Coding

YouTube plays in the background of my workday, attempting to learn and grow while providing intellectual stimulation amidst repetitive, tedius tasks.  A benefit of being an auditory learner is that enough slips through the cracks, tukcing itself away into long term storage to be brought back as a research prompt.  

## From Concept to "Proof of Concept"  
As TechTubers introduce concepts on their channels, they are wise to share what it can do without sharing how to do it.  Layering in LLM's, if we can describe their methodology, we can generate the how-to guide to get started.  These guides (in my limited experience) can get you started with some LLM's more capable at packaging a "finished" product.  From my experience, iteration is still required to make the idea in my head a reality on the screen.  

## Benefits of "Failing Fast"
It is amazing how quickly, as one begins testing, you start to accumulate "filaments that do not work" in your knowledgebase.  GitHub's ability to not only host but execute code was first inroduced as the methodology for this blog ((Network Chuck - I started a blog.....in 20204)[https://youtu.be/dnE7c0ELEH8]).  

Ideas quickly escalate, from generating a personal morning news bulletin to scraping a site for daily changes.  The 2nd, and aggregating data as a concurrent research project, quickly saw me exceeding my allocation of free runtime minutes on GitHub.  What was an up and coming developer to do?  Sign up for a monthly GitHub subscription?  

## Tech Graveyard 

It has gone from a running joke between myself and my wife to a disconcerting reality that my basement is "where tech goes to die".  Prior to our last move, it was time to say goodbye to the original iPhone, a tower with 32bit arthitecture CPU, and a handful of our college era laptops.  Dump/recycling runs have proven to be cathartic and will be explored more in the coming weeks.  

### Frankenputer ... that might need to be a VM name
But all is not a loss.  The basement does house the server which hosted the Tailscale node we leveraged for banking while out of state.  With a linux box, your electric bill, thermal throttling, and hardware bottlenecks are your only limiting factors.  

Unused/underused hardware sitting in the basement (or in this case, heating my basement) can help augment freemium runtime limits.  And so, Matrix style, the brains are swapped out, the harware put back into production, and "It's alive" rings from the heart as the workstation comes online, reading to cp and ./ whatever we can throw at it.
